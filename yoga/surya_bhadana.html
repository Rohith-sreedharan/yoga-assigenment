Surya Bhadana


INTRODUCTION

The Process of breathing in the Surya Bhedana Pranayama is the process of inhaling with the right nostril
and exhaling from the left nostril.
In all the processes of breathing, the energy goes through Pingala or Sunrise.
In this article below, we have provided you information regarding the method of Surya Bhedana Pranayama,
its benefits as well as the precautions associated with this Pranayama.

Steps to follow

1.)At first, sit on the floor in a comfortable Asana or Padmasana (Lotus Pose)

2.)Keep your head and spine straight
Close the eyes and relax your whole body

3.)Put the hands on the knees in the Chin or Gyan Mudra (hold the index finger and thumb together yet lightly while spreading the remaining 3 fingers i.e. middle finger, ring finger, and little finger.)
When the body is in a calm and relaxed state then concentrate on your breath for some time

4.)Then close the left nostril with the fingers of the right-hand finger and slowly inhale from the right nostril
Now close your right nostril to exhale from your left nostril

5.)Again close left nostril to inhale with right nostril and the close right nostril to exhale from the left nostril
Repeat this process for 10 to 15 minutes


NOTE:
The ratio of breathing should be 1:4:2; it means if you inhale for 1 second then hold the breath for 4 seconds and then exhale for 2 seconds.


Time & Duration For Pranayam:

If you practice it every day, you will get good results.
Practicing this pranayama on an empty stomach in the morning and in the evening is more fruitful.
The time period of this pranayama should be gradually increased.
Initially, this pranayama should be done for 4-5 minutes.
After that, it should be done for 10-15 minutes.

Precautions of Pranayam:

1. A person with high BP should not practice the Surya Bhedana pranayama
2. Do not drink anything during the pranayama.
3. Do not eat anything during the pranayama.
4. Heart patients should not practice this Pranayama
5. Inhale only through your right nostril and exhale only through your left nostril
6. The ratio of Inhalation & exhalation should be 1:2.
7. A person who is suffering from fever should not practice this Pranayama
